# The Stacked–Inflationary Expanse Hypothesis

**Author:** Theresa J. Morris  
**Date:** July 4, 2026  
**Status:** Conceptual companion hypothesis; not observationally established

## Purpose

This paper separates and compares four candidate architectures often blended together in popular discussion:

1. inflationary bubble domains
2. stacked or brane-like domains
3. branching cosmic histories
4. landscapes of possible vacuum states

These are not assumed to be one proven theory.

## Candidate geometries

### Bubble domains

Inflating background -> U1 + U2 + U3 + more domains.

### Stacked or brane-like domains

UA / UB / UC as layered candidate domains.

### Branching histories

One prior state -> multiple possible histories.

### Vacuum landscape

A large set of possible vacuum states with different effective properties.

## Merged research architecture

The proposed common question is not whether all four pictures are identical. It is:

**How does a deeper vacuum landscape generate distinct evolving cosmic domains?**

The working architecture is:

Landscape -> vacuum dynamics -> inflation -> cosmic domains -> Tri-Vector histories.

Each domain may have its own history of:

- expansion
- structure growth
- dark-energy behavior

A universe is therefore modeled not merely as one fixed value, but as an evolving trajectory.

## Anthropic extension

The anthropic filter acts on the full trajectory:

**Which cosmic histories permit persistent structure?**

This is richer than asking only which fixed value permits observers.

## Scientific boundary

No evidence currently establishes that universes are literally stacked, routinely merge, or interact as branes. The value of the hypothesis is organizational: it treats bubbles, layers, branches, and landscape states as candidate geometries beneath one common dynamical question.
