## Before 2024

The standard cosmological model treated dark energy as a fixed property of space with an equation-of-state value of w = -1.

Human observers did not watch dark energy continuously for billions of years. Instruments on Earth receive light from objects at different distances and therefore from different cosmic epochs.

Earth today <- light from many cosmic epochs.

By comparing redshifts and standard rulers across distance, modern surveys reconstruct a history of expansion.

## The observational shift

The 2024 DESI results did not prove that dark energy evolves. The measurements remained compatible with the standard cosmological-constant model. However, when DESI data were combined with other cosmological measurements, the possibility of time-evolving dark energy became a serious observational question.

The historical shift is:

Fixed dark-energy assumption -> multi-epoch measurement -> possible evolving-dark-energy signal.

## Central question

Was dark energy ever established as a fixed property of space, or was constancy the best working assumption before measurements became precise enough to test evolution?

The July 4, 2026 Resident Reality answers cautiously:

**Constancy is the standard model. Evolution is an observationally motivated possibility. Neither underlying mechanism is yet finally explained.**
