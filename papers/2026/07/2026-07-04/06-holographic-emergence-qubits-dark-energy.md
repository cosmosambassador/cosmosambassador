# Holographic Emergence, Qubits, and the Dark-Energy Frontier

**Author:** Theresa J. Morris  
**Date:** July 4, 2026  
**Status:** Research synthesis and conceptual extension; not a claim of discovery

## Central idea

Modern physics may be observing different levels of description of one deeper reality. A particle, a quantum field, a spacetime geometry, and an information-theoretic description may not be rival objects at the same scale. They may be different descriptions that become useful at different levels.

The key question is not only: What particle is missing?

It is also: Could space, time, gravity, and dark-energy behavior be collective phenomena that emerge from deeper quantum relations?

## 1997: gravity and lower-dimensional quantum field theory

Juan Maldacena's 1997 AdS/CFT proposal gave a concrete realization of holography. In specific settings, a gravity or string theory in a higher-dimensional anti-de Sitter spacetime is dual to a lower-dimensional conformal quantum field theory.

The lesson is profound:

Higher-dimensional gravity <-> lower-dimensional quantum field theory.

This does not prove that our accelerating universe is holographic in the same way. Our universe is closer to de Sitter-like expansion than anti-de Sitter geometry. But AdS/CFT established that spacetime and gravity can, in special theories, be encoded in a quantum system with fewer spacetime dimensions.

## 2006: geometry and entanglement entropy

Ryu and Takayanagi proposed that entanglement entropy in a boundary quantum field theory is related to the area of a minimal surface in the higher-dimensional gravitational geometry.

Quantum entanglement <-> geometric area.

The importance for an emergent-spacetime worldview is that geometry can reflect patterns of quantum information.

## 2009 to 2012: tensor networks and scale

Tensor-network research connected scale-organized quantum information with emergent holographic geometry.

This is close to the Tri-Vector question: size and scale may be levels of description rather than independent substances.

A single electron is treated by the Standard Model as elementary and pointlike within present experimental reach. But an emergent theory does not require spacetime itself to be elementary merely because the electron is modeled that way.

The scale of description matters.

## 2010: spacetime connectivity from entanglement

Research on holographic spacetime argued that classically connected geometry is closely related to quantum entanglement. Reducing entanglement can correspond to regions of spacetime pulling apart and disconnecting.

More entanglement -> stronger geometric connection.

Less entanglement -> geometric separation.

This does not mean ordinary laboratory qubits have been experimentally shown to manufacture real cosmic space. It means that, in holographic theory, quantum entanglement can act as structural data for geometry.

## 2014 and after: quantum error correction and complexity

Holographic research connected bulk locality with quantum error correction. Information in a gravitational bulk can be redundantly encoded in a lower-dimensional quantum description.

This suggests:

Spacetime locality may be an emergent code property.

Leonard Susskind and others also emphasized that entanglement alone may not explain all aspects of spacetime growth. Complexity and dynamics may matter.

So the modern lesson is not simply:

Space = entanglement.

It is closer to:

Spacetime emergence may require entanglement + organization + dynamics + complexity.

## Heat analogy: collective phenomena

Temperature is real, measurable, and predictive, but it is not a fundamental particle. It emerges from collective microscopic motion.

Likewise, spacetime or gravity might be real and measurable without being fundamental at the deepest level.

The open question is whether gravity, geometry, or dark-energy behavior could also be emergent collective phenomena.

## Why particle searches may not be enough

Particle physics asks whether a new excitation exists.

Emergent physics asks whether a familiar macroscopic effect is produced collectively by many deeper degrees of freedom.

These are different questions.

Dark energy may therefore be a true cosmological constant, a dynamical scalar field, a vacuum residual, a modified-gravity effect, an emergent property of deeper quantum geometry, or something not yet formulated.

No current observation selects one of these as final.

## Dark energy as density

The cosmological-constant model treats dark-energy density as constant per unit physical volume.

As the universe expands, matter density falls because matter is diluted. A constant vacuum density does not dilute in the same way.

This produces the strange implication that a larger physical volume contains more total vacuum energy even though the density remains the same.

The statement 'the universe makes more dark energy' must therefore be handled carefully. In the standard model, it is better to say:

More expanding volume + constant vacuum density -> more total vacuum energy in that larger volume.

The mechanism behind this remains conceptually difficult.

## Exponential future, with an important correction

If dark energy is exactly a positive cosmological constant and remains dominant, the scale factor approaches exponential expansion at late times.

But galaxies do not all expand internally. Gravitationally bound systems such as galaxies, solar systems, and atoms do not simply stretch with the Hubble flow.

The expansion mainly increases distances between sufficiently separated unbound structures.

Cosmic expansion does not mean every object expands.

## The 93-billion-light-year observable sphere and the multiverse

The commonly quoted roughly 93-billion-light-year diameter refers to the present comoving diameter of the observable universe.

That surface is an observational horizon, not a measured physical wall.

In eternal-inflation bubble models, our entire observable universe could lie inside one bubble domain embedded in a much larger inflating multiverse.

Therefore:

93-billion-light-year observable sphere != proven bubble boundary.

The bubble, if such a model describes reality, could extend far beyond our observable horizon.

This distinction is essential to the Resident Reality framework.

## Current frontier: de Sitter holography

Because our observed universe is not anti-de Sitter, researchers are trying to understand whether holographic ideas can be extended to de Sitter-like spacetime.

Recent work studies quantum-information models of de Sitter horizons, entanglement-based connectivity, observer-dependent descriptions, algebraic approaches to emergent time, and candidate definitions of holographic entropy in de Sitter space.

This remains an active frontier, not a completed theory.

## Current dark-energy frontier

As of July 4, 2026, combined cosmological measurements continue to motivate serious tests of evolving dark-energy models. The evidence is not yet a discovery of quintessence or any other mechanism.

This is exactly why the Tri-Vector framework matters.

The measurable question is whether deviations in expansion history, structure growth, and dark-energy behavior form one correlated pattern.

## Tri-Vector extension

The holographic-emergence extension asks:

Could the observable Tri-Vector be a lower-level projection of deeper quantum-geometric dynamics?

The conceptual map is:

Quantum information and field dynamics -> emergent geometry and gravity -> cosmic expansion and structure -> observable Tri-Vector residuals.

The Tri-Vector does not yet derive spacetime from qubits.

It proposes a method:

1. Measure several projections of cosmic behavior.
2. Find a stable correlated residual.
3. Ask whether one deeper quantum or geometric model predicts that residual.
4. Test the next observation before naming a new quantum.

## The Elephant Principle, updated

One field may touch the trunk.

Another may touch the leg.

Another may measure heat, motion, charge, curvature, or entanglement.

The frontier question is whether these measurements are parts of one deeper system.

The new rule is:

**A level of description can be real without being fundamental.**

## Conclusion

From 1997 to 2026, the holographic research program increasingly connected gravity, geometry, entanglement, quantum information, tensor networks, complexity, and error correction.

The strongest conclusion is not that space has already been proven to be made of qubits.

It is that modern theory has given serious mathematical examples in which spacetime and gravity are not the deepest starting variables.

The Tri-Vector research program therefore asks whether dark-energy behavior could belong to the same larger frontier: not necessarily a missing particle, but a measurable collective consequence of deeper quantum structure.

**Research rule:** The whole is not visible from one level of description. Prediction must connect the levels.
