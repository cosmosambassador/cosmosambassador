## Recommended MFA Methods

Preferred MFA methods, from stronger to weaker:

1. Hardware security keys where supported.
2. Passkeys where supported.
3. Authenticator apps.
4. Device-based push approvals.
5. SMS codes only when stronger options are unavailable.

SMS MFA is better than no MFA, but it should not be treated as the strongest option because phone numbers can be vulnerable to SIM-swap and account-recovery attacks.

---

## Recovery Rule

MFA must always include a recovery plan.

Minimum recovery checklist:

1. Save backup codes in a secure offline location.
2. Keep recovery email and phone information current.
3. Record which authenticator app or hardware key protects each account.
4. Use at least two recovery paths for critical accounts.
5. Avoid storing all recovery data only inside the same account being protected.

## Public Education Version

Multi-Factor Authentication means proving identity with more than one factor.

Common factors include:

- Something you know, such as a password.
- Something you have, such as a phone, authenticator app, passkey, or hardware key.
- Something you are, such as device biometrics used locally by a trusted device.

For ACO and AISI.services, MFA is not optional decoration. It is a baseline security habit for anyone managing domains, websites, publishing accounts, cloud services, AI tools, media channels, or payment systems.
